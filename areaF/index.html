Grand Staircase at Area F

Roman architecture is not only concerned with the exterior of their structures, but the interior as well. One structure that can be on a buildingâ€™s interior or exterior is the staircase. The staircase here is part of a large stone building, called Area F, dating back to between 350 and 250 BC. Archaeologists digging at Gabii are unsure of the purpose of the building structure. It was possibly a public building, yet it may have also been a private residence. Whether a community space or a private home, the staircase is part of an elaborate structure.

The stones used in Area F weigh thousands of pounds each. They are stacked on top of each other without any type of mortar. The staircase in Area F is very large and connects two terraces. The Area F staircase is one of the earliest known cases of altering the natural landscape for the sake of Roman architecture. The staircase is next to a wall, both of which alter the natural slope of the hill that Area F rests against.

Purpose of Area F

Was Area F a public space or private residence? Academics support both hypotheses about the purpose of Area F.
